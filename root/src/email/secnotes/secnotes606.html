Coast to Coast run – Epic! And Sunday Scramble

What a huge success! Overnight leading into Drive it Day in April saw the first CT Coast to Coast run. This event was in Lieu of the Home Counties Run, (HCR) which has sadly suffered dwindling support in recent years. The C2C event was sold out! Starting at Anglesey Race Circuit was an inspired choice as the venue, staff and even the food were all excellent. The sighting laps were fun and left me wanting more. The route across country was well planned by the organisers and was easily achievable within the time limits. I missed some of the ‘fun’ elements of the HCR and the extra camaraderie at the finish but that’s just a personal note. Well done to Chris Shaw, Ellis, Dale and Nigel.

OlaF and Evie. Running reports.

Well following on from my last report I’ve used up most of the polish I bought at the time of my last message, but not on Olaf or Evie. I have been helping to get Chris Banks’s Mk 2 2000 auto ready for the road. Chris and Tony Pulis carried out a suspension and brake system rebuild last year and Chris has refurbed the wheels and fitted new tyres to the car that had spent it’s last few years in a barn on the Isle of Wight, It had been parked up in a seaside car park before being discovered some years later in a barn by Angela Cotton and her partner Graham who introduced the car to Chris. My involvement has entailed a lot of fettling and fiddling and the fitting of a new distributor, all rubber hoses and pipes, all belts, an alternator and a myriad of other parts. The biggest problem of all was a blocked fuel line which finally came to notice after several failed attempts to cure the poor fuel flow involving replacement mechanical pumps, electrical pumps and various other idea’s. the final diagnosis involved a flap of PTFE! This had been used on the fuel tank union and turned out to be the cause of much head scratching and mirth on the part of Mr and Mrs Banks who had no faith that Chris’s car would ever leave their front drive under its own steam again.

My promise to report back on some suspect parts can only be partially reported on as I received a refund from Mr Rimmer for the brake drums but I have yet to resolve the starter motor issue. Olaf suffered a big puncture and has had a new tyre fitted and I took the opportunity of fitting a matching one to the spare wheel that I picked up at Stoneleigh. A visit to the Beaulieu Spring Auto Jumble yielded a bit of a haul of body parts and I now have an almost full ‘Kit’ ready for Olaf’s big rebuild after the RBRR. More next time.


French Tour - Dates

The CT Scenic and Continental French Tour will take place between June 4th -11th June 2019. Late advice meant that the part of the trip that includes two nights in Verdon has been upgraded to half board at a spa resort. The final price is £879 per head. This is a lot of money I know but please see the itinerary on the Club Forum or Facebook and in the article in next month’s mag. This is a big tour over 8 days/7 nights covering over 2000 miles and takes in a visit to Le Mans, with time to drive the public part of the circuit and visit the museum, onward to the war memorial at the abandoned Village at Oradour sur Glane, a full Millau bridge experience and some of the most spectacular scenery France has to offer in the Volcanoes National Park and the Spectacular Gorges du Verdon where there will be a day of down time before the drive home which includes a visit to the old Grand Prix circuit at Reims. Ferries and 3/ 4 Star hotels (B+B) are included as well as road books giving alternate routes and the holiday is fully ABTA backed. I hope for more and the tour will be open to all Standard Triumph owners. Other Classic cars are also welcome. Come on let’s have some fun! It’s quite a hike to the deep south but the driving should be well within everybody’s distance capabilities and the memories will be epic!


There are a maximum of ten places available on the trip. Six have confirmed and a further one is likely if you fancy the trip please contact me and I will forward your details. Thank you.
